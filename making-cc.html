How Skins Work in Sims 1

Key concepts before you start – very different from later games

⚠️ Sims 1 CC works completely differently from Sims 4. There are no separate tops, bottoms or hair items. Each outfit is a single flat texture painted onto the body mesh. Understanding this first saves a lot of confusion.
👕 Clothing = body skin
A Sim's body and clothing are one combined texture file. There's no separate top or bottom layer – each outfit is a complete body image painted onto the 3D mesh.
💇 Hair = head skin
Hair is painted directly onto the head texture. It's not a separate item – you change hairstyle by changing the whole head file. The face and hair are one image.
🎨 Three skin tones
Sims 1 has light, medium and dark skin tones. If your outfit exposes skin, you need three separate files – one per tone – with the matching base skin colour underneath.
👥 Three body types + child
Skinny (default), fit (athletic), fat (plus-size) – each in male and female. Children have their own mesh. A skin made for a fit body looks wrong on a skinny body.
⚠️ 256-colour BMP only
All body and head textures must be saved as Indexed (256-colour) BMP files. Regular 24-bit BMPs, PNGs or JPGs show as a white blank body in game. This step is critical.

Tools Overview

What each tool does and where to get it

A note on Legacy Collection compatibility: the original Maxis tools (The Sims Creator, Home Crafter) need a registry key that Legacy Collection doesn't create. Each subpage has detailed setup instructions. The Secondhand-Feraligatr installer handles this automatically.
